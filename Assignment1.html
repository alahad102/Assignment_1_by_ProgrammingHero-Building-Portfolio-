Research Experience

Undergrad Thesis | Targeted Face Recognition from Video and Alarm Generation for Security Concern

Face recognition has been considered one of the most important means of security in prevention of crimes in this era. Implementation of face recognition has led to reduction in crime rates in major cities around the world. Surveillance cameras in crowded areas keeps good monitoring of all activities. So it can be used as a witness against criminals or can be used to prevent crime before happening. With the advancement and research in deep neural networks in surveillance cameras, face recognition accuracy has increased in challenging environments too. But our country is still lagging behind in this regard. By making a security system that can recognize the face of a person wanted by the law enforcement authority, positive steps are taken towards the benefit of the society. So the proposed work focuses mainly on face recognition with custom Bangladeshi dataset that can be robust enough against blurriness, pose variations and occlusions. Single Shot Multibox Detector (SSD) model was chosen since it produced significant improvement in accuracy compared to many state of the art models. Tensorflow API was used with SSD Mobilenet FPN model config to generate alarms when targeted face was recognized among many faces in crowd.
